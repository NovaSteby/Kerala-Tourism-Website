KOZHIKODE

Kozhikode, historically known as Calicut, is a vibrant coastal city situated on the Malabar Coast of Kerala, India. Renowned for its rich maritime history and bustling trade routes, Kozhikode was once a prominent spice trading hub and a gateway to the Indian subcontinent for traders from around the world. The city's historic significance is reflected in its landmarks, such as the iconic Mananchira Square, where the grandeur of the Zamorin rulers' era is still palpable, and the ancient Mishkal Mosque, an architectural marvel dating back to the 14th century.

Today, Kozhikode retains its cultural allure with its bustling markets, vibrant streets, and delectable cuisine. The city's bustling markets, including the iconic SM Street (Sweet Meat Street), offer a delightful array of spices, textiles, and traditional handicrafts, creating a vibrant tapestry of sights, sounds, and aromas. Additionally, Kozhikode's culinary heritage, epitomized by its lip-smacking Malabar cuisine, with its aromatic biryanis, flavorful seafood dishes, and delectable sweets like the famous Kozhikode halwa, continues to entice food enthusiasts from far and wide, making it a gastronomic paradise on the Malabar Coast.
