ALAPPUZHA

Alappuzha, often referred to as Alleppey, is a picturesque town situated in the southern part of Kerala, India. Renowned for its intricate network of backwaters, serene beaches, and lush greenery, Alappuzha is a popular tourist destination and is often referred to as the "Venice of the East."

The annual Nehru Trophy Boat Race, held on the Punnamada Lake in Alappuzha, is a major highlight of the region's cultural calendar. This thrilling event, featuring snake boat races and colorful cultural performances, attracts thousands of spectators from around the world. With its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and warm hospitality, Alappuzha offers visitors an enchanting glimpse into the heart and soul of Kerala. Whether you're seeking relaxation amidst serene backwaters or adventure along pristine beaches, Alappuzha has something for everyone to enjoy.
